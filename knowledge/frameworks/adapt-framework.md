# A.D.A.P.T. Framework — Full Reference

## Overview

A.D.A.P.T. is the QA framework for analyzing and creating yapper scripts. Every script must map to all five components.

---

## A — Authority Hook & Attention (0-5 seconds of a 1:00-3:30 script)

**Purpose:** Disrupt the scroll in the first 5 seconds.

**Components:**

1. **Target demographic identification**
   - "Ladies over 50"
   - "42 years old, and I've been using..."

2. **Credibility establishment**
   - Professional title: "I'm a certified injector"
   - Long-term use: "8 months of using"
   - Results claim: "turning my skin from this to this"

3. **Pattern interrupt**
   - Contrarian statement: "That fancy electric toothbrush... might be aging you"
   - Shocking claim: "Someone's about to get fired because..."
   - Personal confession: "I felt like a fraud"

---

## D — Define the Enemy (5-20 seconds)

**Purpose:** Create contrast between the status quo and your solution.

**Three Types of Enemy Attacks:**

1. **Cost/Access Attack**
   - "Microneedling professionally is not always accessible or affordable"
   - Anchor to expensive alternative, show yours is affordable

2. **Efficacy/Risk Attack**
   - "My drugstore one only does 7,000 vibrations... apparently it's not enough"
   - "You're brushing with toilet germs"

3. **Skepticism Attack**
   - "I thought there's no way it's as good as they say"
   - "I kept seeing affiliates promoting it and thought it was hype"

---

## A — Anecdotal Catalyst (20-45 seconds)

**Purpose:** Use a personal story to create belief transfer.

**Key Elements:**

1. **Vulnerability/Doubt**
   - "I felt like a fraud"
   - "I had no idea what this stuff was"
   - "I thought it was probably super expensive"

2. **External Proof Source**
   - Authority: "My dentist said..."
   - Peer: "My neighbor Sarah... perfect teeth at 60"
   - Family: "My daughter said 'your skin looks better than ever'"
   - Client results: "One client dropped 6 pounds in 3 weeks"

3. **Discovery Moment**
   - "That's when it hit me"
   - "I finally bothered to look into it"
   - "I was immediately intrigued"

**The Pattern:** "I was skeptical → [Credible person] showed me results → I tried it → My experience validated it"

---

## P — Proof & Unique Mechanism (45s-2:00)

**Three Mechanism Types (choose based on market sophistication):**

### 1. Unspoken Mechanism ("It's Toasted")
State something true that nobody else bothers to say:
- "Our serum absorbs in under 30 seconds" (most do — but saying it feels exclusive)
- "Triple-filtered for purity"
- Works best in **unsophisticated markets**

### 2. Unique Problem Mechanism (UPM)
Reframe WHY the problem exists:
- "It's not bad skin — your cleanser is destroying your skin barrier every morning"
- "You don't have a weight problem — you have a cortisol problem"
- Works best in **developing markets**

### 3. Solution Mechanism (UMS — Unique Mechanism Simplified)
Translate technical features into simple, visual language:
- Technical: "41,000 sonic vibrations per minute"
- Yapper: "Way more powerful but somehow gentler"

- Technical: "UV sterilization base"
- Yapper: "That cleans it with UV light while it charges — you can't afford to brush with toilet germs"

- Technical: "AHA/BHA exfoliation"
- Yapper: "Scratches the surface of your skin so it absorbs everything better"

**Pattern:** [What it does] → [Why that matters to them]

### UMP (Unique Marketing Point — Sensory)
Use visceral, sensory words to describe effects:
- "My teeth felt SMOOOOTH"
- "Skin absolutely BOUNCY"
- "Almost feels like wee little pieces of glass"
- "This redness — that's what you want to see"

### Objection Validation
Turn potential negatives into positives:
- "This redness, that's what you want to see. It's creating collagen production."

---

## T — Transition and Call-to-Action (2:00-3:00+)

**Three-Part Structure:**

### 1. Value Overload
Stack the benefits visually:
- "You're getting 8 full-size products"
- "Worth $228... under $80 plus free shipping"
- "When you buy 2 months, you get 2 months FREE"

### 2. Scarcity Stacking
Combine time + inventory urgency:
- Time: "Right now," "This deal," "Just today"
- Inventory: "Going to sell out," "I don't know how long this will last"
- Combined: "Crazy deal right now... bundles are going to sell out"

### 3. Final Command + Safety Net
- Command: "Go get yours," "Click below," "I'm linking it there"
- Safety: "So you don't grab a knockoff like I did"
- Validation: "100 times over, it is worth it"

**CTA Formulas:**
- "I'm linking it [location] + [validation]"
- "[Urgency statement] + [command] + [safety net]"
- "[Value restatement] + [scarcity] + [command]"

## Script Templates by Product Type

### Template 1: Problem-Solution (Default)

**HOOK (0-5s):** "[PROBLEM QUESTION]? I'm [AGE/PROFESSION], and this was [EMOTIONAL REACTION]."

**SETUP (5-20s):** "I would [WHAT YOU TRIED THAT DIDN'T WORK]. I thought [LOGICAL ASSUMPTION], but turns out [HIDDEN TRUTH]."

**CATALYST (20-45s):** "Then [EXTERNAL SOURCE with CREDIBILITY] told me about [PRODUCT]. [THEIR RESULTS were SPECIFIC]. I was skeptical but decided to try it."

**PROOF (45s-2:00):** "[YOUR IMMEDIATE SENSORY RESULTS]. Here's why it works: [MECHANISM SIMPLIFIED]. [SPECIFIC RESULTS with TIMEFRAME]. That's when I realized [CORE INSIGHT]."

**CLOSE (2:00-3:00):** "Now I [TRANSFORMATION]. [VALUE STACK]. [URGENCY]. [CTA]. [SAFETY NET]."

### Template 2: Skincare/Transformation Timeline

**HOOK (0-5s):** "[AGE] and I've been using [PRODUCT] for [TIMEFRAME], here are my honest thoughts. My only regret is I didn't buy it sooner."

**SETUP (5-25s):** "I kept seeing [SKEPTICISM SOURCE] and thought there's no way. But then [CREDIBILITY MARKER] and I had to try it. [ROUTINE WALKTHROUGH — show the product in use]."

**PROOF (25s-1:30):** "[TIMEFRAME BREAKDOWN]:
- In 1 minute: [IMMEDIATE EFFECT — sensory]
- After 1 week: [VISIBLE CHANGE]
- By day 10: [DEEPER CHANGE]
- In 2 weeks: [COMPLETE TRANSFORMATION]"

**VALIDATION (1:30-2:00):** "[EXTERNAL VALIDATION: daughter/friend compliment]. [DEEPER PRODUCT DETAILS — ingredients, what makes it different]."

**CLOSE (2:00-3:00):** "The bundle is [VALUE], under [PRICE], free shipping. [ITEMIZE WHAT THEY GET]. I'm linking it — 100 times over worth it."

### Template 3: Authority/A-B Test

**HOOK (0-5s):** "As a [PROFESSION], [FRUSTRATING PATTERN] was driving me [EMOTIONAL REACTION]."

**SETUP (5-25s):** "I had [COMPARISON SCENARIO — identical situations]. One [OUTCOME A], the other [OUTCOME B]. Made me feel like [VULNERABLE ADMISSION]. [EXPAND on the professional context — what you see daily, why this matters]."

**CATALYST (25-50s):** "Then [EXTERNAL SOURCE] shows up with [PRODUCT]. [THEIR CREDIBILITY/RESULTS]. I tried it myself: [YOUR IMMEDIATE RESULTS]. So I tested it with [A/B SCENARIO]."

**PROOF (50s-1:45):** "The difference was [SPECIFIC RESULTS]. That's when I realized [HIDDEN TRUTH]. Most people think [COMMON BELIEF], but [CORRECT UNDERSTANDING]. [DETAILED MECHANISM — explain how/why it works differently]."

**CLOSE (1:45-2:30):** "Now [TRANSFORMATION to BUSINESS/APPROACH]. [URGENCY/DEAL]. I don't get paid to recommend this — just tired of seeing [FRUSTRATION] when the solution is so simple."

### Template 4: Value Bundle Showcase

**HOOK (0-5s):** "Someone [DRAMATIC REACTION] at [BRAND] because there's no way this is [RETAIL VALUE] for [ACTUAL PRICE] plus [BONUS]."

**ITEMIZATION (5-1:30):** "This is their [BUNDLE NAME]. You're getting:
- [ITEM 1] — [UNIQUE BENEFIT/DESCRIPTOR + show product]
- [ITEM 2] — the viral [VIRAL ELEMENT + demonstrate]
- [ITEM 3] — this is [SIMPLIFIED MECHANISM + texture/feel]
- [ITEM 4] — [SENSORY DESCRIPTION + visual demo]
- [ITEM 5+] — [Continue itemizing with mini-demos for each]"

**VALUE STACK (1:30-2:15):** "This is everything you need for [OUTCOME]. [RETAIL VALUE] worth of product for [ACTUAL PRICE], free shipping. [BREAK DOWN the per-item value]."

**CLOSE (2:15-3:00):** "This is actually an amazing deal. I don't know how long it's going to last, but these bundles are going to sell out. I'm linking it right there — go get yours."

## Emotional Triggers Reference

### Life Force 8 (Drew Whitman)
1. Survival, life extension
2. Enjoyment of food/beverages
3. Freedom from fear, pain, danger
4. Sexual companionship
5. Comfortable living conditions
6. To be superior, winning
7. Care for loved ones
8. Social approval

### Mindstates (Sarah Levinger)
1. Autonomy — "I don't get paid to recommend this"
2. Achievement — "Finally breaking through plateaus"
3. Belonging — "Thousands of women have experienced this"
4. Competence — "The hidden problem most people don't realize"
5. Esteem — "Looking 10 years younger"
6. Engagement — "The most beneficial anti-aging treatment"
7. Empowerment — "You don't need expensive spa treatments"
8. Nurturance — "Care for your aging skin"
9. Security — "60-day money-back guarantee"

### How to Choose
1. Identify the product's core transformation
2. Ask "What does this transformation enable them to FEEL?"
3. Match to the deepest emotional driver
4. Build the story around that emotion

## Awareness Level → Lead Type

| Awareness Level | Lead Type | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Unaware | Story Lead | Slow reveal of problem + solution |
| Problem-Aware | Proclamation Lead | "Why the Chinese NEVER get sick!" |
| Solution-Aware | Problem-Solution Lead | "Do you wake up to pee multiple times a night?" |
| Product-Aware | Promise Lead | "Grow your hair 10x quicker with this magical serum!" |
| Most-Aware | Offer Lead | "Buy our bestselling pants now, 20% OFF!" |

Default to **Solution-Aware** if not specified. This has the best balance of audience size and conversion potential.

## QA Checklist

### Before Writing:
- [ ] Avatar built desire-first (Core 5 Categories)
- [ ] Emotion identified (Life Force 8 or Mindstate)
- [ ] Awareness level determined
- [ ] Lead type selected
- [ ] Big Domino identified (the ONE belief we need accepted)
- [ ] Product mechanism understood (can explain in one simple sentence)
- [ ] Mechanism type chosen (Unspoken / UPM / Solution)

### A — Authority Hook (0-5s):
- [ ] Target demographic clearly identified
- [ ] Pattern interrupt present
- [ ] Credibility established
- [ ] Promise or tease clear

### D — Define the Enemy (5-15s):
- [ ] Status quo identified and attacked
- [ ] Cost/access or efficacy attack present
- [ ] Emotional contrast created

### A — Anecdotal Catalyst (15-25s):
- [ ] Vulnerability/doubt included
- [ ] External proof source introduced
- [ ] Discovery moment present
- [ ] Source credibility established

### P — Proof & Mechanism (25-35s):
- [ ] UMS simplified (10-year-old could understand)
- [ ] UMP sensory language included
- [ ] Specific results mentioned (numbers, timeframes)
- [ ] Objections addressed

### T — Transition & CTA (35-45s):
- [ ] Value stacked (itemized benefits)
- [ ] Price anchored
- [ ] Scarcity present (time + inventory)
- [ ] CTA clear and commanding
- [ ] Safety net included

### Overall:
- [ ] Conversational tone maintained throughout
- [ ] Zero confusion
- [ ] Product shown/mentioned frequently
- [ ] Entertainment value — would I watch this?
- [ ] Memorable WOW moment
- [ ] Watch time optimized (rehooks every 5-7 seconds)
- [ ] Pain layered (surface → deeper → deepest)
- [ ] Passes "Don't Talk, Only Point" (Visualize / Falsify / Unique)
- [ ] Gender-appropriate tone (validation for women, challenge for men)
- [ ] Big Domino attacked throughout
- [ ] Golden Question: "Would I watch this if I wasn't being paid to?"
