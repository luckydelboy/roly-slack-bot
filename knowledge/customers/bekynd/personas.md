## **Persona 1: "Sarah \- The Medical Treatment Dropout"**

### **Demographics & Background**

* **Age**: 38 years old  
* **Occupation**: Marketing Manager at mid-size tech company  
* **Location**: Suburban Atlanta, GA  
* **Income**: $75,000 annually  
* **Family Status**: Married with two teenagers  
* **Hair Type**: Fine, straight brown hair that shows flakes easily

### **Awareness Level: Problem Aware \+ Solution Aware (Level 4\)**

Sarah knows exactly what her condition is (scalp psoriasis diagnosed 5 years ago) and has extensively researched available solutions. She's tried multiple medical treatments and is now seeking alternatives. She's aware that most traditional approaches haven't worked for her and is open to trying natural solutions, though she remains somewhat skeptical due to past disappointments.

### **Main Desires (Ranked by Intensity)**

1. **Freedom from treatment dependency cycles** \- "I'm tired of products that work for 3 months then stop"  
2. **Professional confidence restoration** \- "I need to feel confident in client meetings again"  
3. **Sustainable long-term scalp health** \- "I want something that will work for years, not months"  
4. **Social wardrobe freedom** \- "I miss wearing my favorite black blazers without worry"  
5. **Natural solution that actually works** \- "I want to heal my scalp without harsh chemicals"

### **Dream Outcome**

Sarah's ideal scenario is waking up without the automatic head scratch check, being able to wear any color clothing to work, and having a simple weekly routine that maintains her scalp health indefinitely. She dreams of running her hands through her hair during stressful meetings without worry about visible flakes, and feeling confident enough to get professional headshots updated for the first time in three years.

### **Specific Hopes**

* **Short-term (2-4 weeks)**: Noticeable reduction in daily itching and flaking  
* **Medium-term (2-3 months)**: Complete elimination of embarrassing flakes, return to normal hair washing schedule  
* **Long-term (6+ months)**: Sustained scalp health without constant product switching, feeling proud of her hair again  
* **Professional**: Confidence in video calls and client presentations without scalp distraction  
* **Personal**: Being able to wear her husband's dark t-shirts to bed without leaving evidence

### **Specific Fears**

* **Treatment tolerance**: "What if this works initially but then stops like everything else?"  
* **Ingredient reactions**: "Will natural ingredients cause unexpected allergic reactions?"  
* **Financial waste**: "I've already spent $800+ on failed treatments this year"  
* **Social judgment**: "What if my team notices my scalp issues during our upcoming product launch?"  
* **Worsening condition**: "What if stopping my prescription makes everything worse?"  
* **Time investment**: "I don't have energy for another complicated routine that fails"

### **Specific Pain Points**

* **Daily morning scalp check** \- automatically running hands through hair to assess overnight flaking  
* **Clothing color limitations** \- avoiding dark colors for important meetings and social events  
* **Shower anxiety** \- dreading hair wash days because of post-wash flaking  
* **Product cabinet overwhelm** \- bathroom filled with failed treatments creating daily frustration  
* **Partner impact** \- husband finding flakes on pillowcases and feeling self-conscious  
* **Professional image concerns** \- avoiding overhead lighting in conference rooms  
* **Seasonal worsening** \- winter months bringing increased severity and stress

### **Failed Solutions History**

1. **Prescription coal tar shampoo** (18 months) \- worked initially but caused hair thinning and smell issues  
2. **Topical steroids** (2 years intermittent) \- effective but developed tolerance, concerned about long-term use  
3. **Clobetasol scalp solution** (6 months) \- expensive, required prescription refills, stopped working  
4. **Head & Shoulders Clinical Strength** (multiple attempts) \- temporary relief but hair became brittle  
5. **Nizoral antifungal shampoo** (8 months) \- helped some but never fully cleared condition  
6. **T/Gel therapeutic shampoo** (1 year) \- messy, smelly, inconsistent results  
7. **Luxury salon treatments** ($300+ spent) \- relaxing but no lasting improvement  
8. **Essential oil DIY treatments** (3 months) \- time-intensive with minimal results

### **Core Beliefs**

* **Medical treatments eventually fail**: "Prescriptions work temporarily but always stop being effective"  
* **Natural solutions are gentler but often weak**: "I want to believe in natural products but they usually don't work for serious conditions"  
* **Scalp health affects overall confidence**: "When my scalp is bad, everything else feels off"  
* **Quality ingredients matter**: "I'm willing to pay more for something that actually works and won't damage my hair"  
* **Companies exaggerate claims**: "I've been burned too many times by products that promise everything"  
* **Community recommendations are trustworthy**: "Real people's experiences matter more than marketing"  
* **Consistency is key**: "I need something I can stick with long-term"  
* **Professional image matters**: "People judge you based on appearance whether they admit it or not"

### **Shopping Behavior**

* Spends 2-3 hours researching before any scalp product purchase  
* Reads minimum 20 reviews and cross-references multiple sources  
* Prefers brands with detailed ingredient explanations  
* Values money-back guarantees as purchase insurance  
* Shops primarily online to avoid in-store product questioning
