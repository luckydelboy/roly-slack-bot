# BeKynd Beauty Customer Personas

## PERSONA 1: Sarah Thompson, 34, Marketing Manager

### CATEGORY 1: DESIRES (Ranked by intensity 1-5)

**Level 1 (Surface):** "I want my scalp to stop itching and flaking"
**Level 2 (Deeper):** "I want to stop feeling self-conscious about white flakes on my dark clothes"  
**Level 3 (Deepest):** "I want to stop feeling like my body is betraying me and that I'm somehow 'dirty' or unhealthy"

**Ranked Desires:**
1. Stop feeling embarrassed in professional/social settings (5/5)
2. Find a solution that actually works long-term (5/5)
3. Wear dark colors confidently again (4/5)
4. Stop the constant itch-scratch cycle (4/5)
5. Feel "normal" and healthy again (4/5)
6. Stop spending money on failed treatments (3/5)
7. Have a simple, effective routine (3/5)

### CATEGORY 2: EXPERIENCES

**Situational Experiences:**
- Started noticing scalp issues around age 30, coinciding with increased work stress
- Worsened during high-pressure project launches and life changes (marriage, new role)
- Pandemic stress made condition significantly worse
- Doctor visits felt dismissive - "just use Head & Shoulders"

**Failed Solutions History:**
1. **Head & Shoulders** (6 months) → Dried out hair, temporary relief only → Felt frustrated, like trying a band-aid
2. **Prescription steroid cream** (3 months) → Worked initially then stopped, hair thinning → Worried about long-term effects  
3. **Tea tree oil treatments** (2 months) → Too harsh, made scalp more irritated → Felt defeated by "natural" solutions failing
4. **Expensive dermatologist shampoo** (4 months) → $45/bottle, minimal improvement → Felt ripped off and desperate
5. **Nizoral** (2 months) → Harsh smell, dried hair, temporary results → Embarrassed by medicinal smell
6. **DIY coconut oil masks** (1 month) → Made buildup worse, attracted more flakes → Felt stupid for trying Pinterest remedies
7. **Selsun Blue** (3 months) → Stripped hair color, barely helped scalp → Angry about ruining expensive highlights

### CATEGORY 3: EMOTIONS

**Primary Emotions:**
- **Embarrassment** (triggered by flakes on clothing, people noticing scratching)
- **Frustration** (triggered by failed solutions, spending money with no results)
- **Anxiety** (triggered by important meetings/dates, wondering if people notice)
- **Shame** (triggered by feeling "dirty" or unhealthy despite good hygiene)
- **Hope** (triggered by new potential solutions, success stories)
- **Anger** (triggered by dismissive medical advice, expensive failed products)

**Secondary Emotions:**
- **Self-consciousness** when wearing dark clothing
- **Relief** when finding products that offer even temporary improvement
- **Stress** about the condition worsening during important life events

### CATEGORY 4: BEHAVIORS

**Research Habits:**
- Spends 2-3 hours researching before trying new products
- Reads reviews obsessively, looking for people with similar symptoms
- Screenshots ingredient lists to compare products
- Joins Facebook groups and Reddit communities for scalp conditions

**Shopping Patterns:**
- Reads every single review, especially 1-3 star reviews
- Waits for sales/bundles due to previous expensive failures
- Prefers brands with money-back guarantees
- Orders multiple products at once when finding a promising brand

**Coping Mechanisms:**
- Strategic outfit planning (light colors, patterns that hide flakes)
- Carries lint rollers and wears scarves
- Avoids certain hairstyles that make scalp more visible
- Washes hair daily hoping to control flakes

**Social Behavior:**
- Doesn't discuss condition with friends/family (too embarrassing)
- Seeks anonymous online communities for advice and support
- Makes excuses to avoid activities where scalp might be more noticeable

### CATEGORY 5: DEMOGRAPHICS

- Age: 34, Female
- Occupation: Marketing Manager at mid-size company
- Location: Suburban areas, major cities
- Income: $65K-85K annually
- Life Stage: Married, no children yet but planning
- Education: College-educated professional
